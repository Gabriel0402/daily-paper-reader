Abstract 

Although logit quantal response equilibrium (logit QRE) offers a natural equilibrium selection mechanism and converges to Nash equilibrium as the rationality parameter tends to infinity, its computation in extensive-form games is generally intractable when based on the normal-form representation, due to the exponential growth of the strategy space. To address this difficulty, this paper develops a sequence-form formulation of logit QRE for finite n-player extensive-form games with perfect recall, which avoids explicit construction of the normal form and enables com-pact computation. Based on this formulation, we further develop a differentiable path-following method starting from an arbitrary initial point, such that each point on the path corresponds to a logit QRE associated with a particular value of the rationality parameter, and the limiting point yields a Nash equilibrium. In this way, the proposed method provides an efficient computa-tional framework for exploiting the equilibrium selection property of logit QRE in extensive-form games. The effectiveness of the proposed method is validated by theoretical analysis and numerical experiments. 

Keywords: Extensive-Form Game, Sequence Form, Nash equilibrium, Logit Quantal Response Equilibrium, Differentiable Path-Following Method 

JEL Classification: C72 

# 1 Introduction 

Extensive-form games provide a standard framework for modeling sequential strategic interactions and have been widely applied in economics, political science, and engineering. An extensive-form game can be represented in normal form by specifying, for each player, a complete contingent plan over 1

all information sets. Therefore, solution concepts originally defined for normal-form games can also be applied to extensive-form games. Among them, Nash equilibrium is one of the most fundamental solution concepts, describing a strategy profile in which no player can improve its payoff through unilateral deviation. However, in dynamic settings, Nash equilibrium may admit outcomes supported by non-credible threats, which limits its descriptive and predictive power. To address this issue, a large body of research has focused on equilibrium refinement and equilibrium selection. Logistic QRE offers an important approach to equilibrium selection by modeling boundedly ratio-nal behavior. Instead of assuming perfectly rational best responses, logistic QRE assumes that actions with higher expected payoffs are chosen with higher probabilities through a logistic response rule. The rationality parameter ╬╗ characterizes the playersÔÇÖ sensitivity to payoff differences, and the cor-responding logistic QRE approaches a Nash equilibrium as ╬╗ Ôćĺ Ôł× . Despite its appealing theoretical properties, computing logistic QRE in the normal-form representation is generally intractable for large extensive-form games, due to the exponential growth of the strategy space. To overcome this difficulty, this paper develops a sequence-form characterization of logistic QRE that avoids explicit expansion of the normal form and yields a compact representation for computation. On this basis, a smooth solution path with respect to the rationality parameter is established, and the path is shown to converge to a Nash equilibrium. The proposed method improves the computational tractability of logistic QRE for extensive-form games. Logistic QRE can be interpreted as an equilibrium selection mechanism based on path following: each point on the solution path corresponds to a logistic QRE associated with a particular value of the rationality parameter, and the limit of the path yields a Nash equilibrium. Under this interpretation, the computation of logistic QRE is naturally related to the literature on path-following methods for equilibrium computation. Path-following methods have been widely studied for normal-form games. Early work includes the complementary pivoting procedure of Lemke and Howson [1], together with its extensions to n-player games by Rosenm┬Ę uller [2] and Wilson [3]. This line of research was later enriched by simplicial methods and their advances, which improved practical implementability [4ÔÇô7]. More recently, differentiable path-following methods have been developed to further improve compu-tational performance, including the differentiable homotopy method [8], the piecewise differentiable global Newton method [9], and the smooth reformulation approach [10]. By exploiting the selection properties of NashÔÇÖs mappings, HarsanyiÔÇÖs tracing procedure, and logistic QRE, Cao and Dang [11ÔÇô 13] further developed corresponding variants and differentiable path-following methods for selecting an exact perfect equilibrium in normal-form games. In addition, differentiable path-following meth-ods have been successfully applied to the computation of other equilibrium concepts [14, 15], showing strong potential as a general computational framework for equilibrium analysis. The sequence form [16ÔÇô18] provides a compact representation of extensive-form games that preserves their sequential structure, and has become an important foundation for equilibrium com-putation. By replacing pure strategies with action sequences and realization plans, it reduces the problem size to one that grows linearly with the game tree, thereby avoiding the exponential blow-up of the normal-form representation. Although logit QRE provides a natural equilibrium selection mechanism, an efficient path-following method for computing Nash equilibria of extensive-form games based on this mechanism is still lacking. To fill this gap, this paper develops a sequence-formÔÇôbased smooth path-following method for finite n-player extensive-form games with perfect recall. By avoiding the explicit con-struction of the exponentially large normal form, the proposed method enables efficient computation of the logit QRE path and its limiting Nash equilibrium. The effectiveness of the proposed method is validated by both theoretical analysis and numerical experiments. The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. Section 2 introduces the preliminaries on extensive-form games, sequence form, and logit QRE. Section 3 presents the sequence-form formulation of logit QRE. Section 4 devel-ops a differentiable path-following method starting from an arbitrary initial point. Section 5 reports numerical experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method. Section 6 concludes the paper.
