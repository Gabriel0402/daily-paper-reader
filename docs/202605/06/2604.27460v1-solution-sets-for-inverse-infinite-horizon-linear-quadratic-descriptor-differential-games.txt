# Solution Sets for Inverse Infinite-Horizon Linear-Quadratic Descriptor Differential Games 

Aaditya Kumar and Puduru Viswanadha Reddy, Member, IEEE 

Abstract ‚ÄĒ In this letter, we study a model-based in-verse problem for infinite-horizon linear-quadratic differen-tial games with descriptor dynamics. Specifically, we seek to identify the set of all cost functions that rationalize an observed feedback strategy profile of the players as a feedback Nash equilibrium, referred to here as the solution set. We characterize the solution set, show that it is rectan-gular and convex, and provide an algorithm to compute an admissible realization. Finally, we illustrate our results with numerical examples. 

Index Terms ‚ÄĒ Game theory; inverse differential games; descriptor systems; feedback Nash equilibrium 

I. I NTRODUCTION 

Dynamic game theory (DGT) provides a mathematical framework for analyzing decision problems involving multi-agent strategic interactions that evolve over time. It has en-abled the systematic study of such problems across engineer-ing, economics, and other fields; see [1]‚Äď[3]. More recently, advances in data-driven methods have led to renewed interest in inverse dynamic games (IDGs), which seek to infer agents‚Äô objectives from observed behavioral data. Such methods en-able the recovery of agents‚Äô preferences and the design of mechanisms that are consistent with real-world observations. The practical relevance of IDGs has been demonstrated in several domains, including human‚Äďmachine shared control [4], [5], autonomous driving [6], [7], biological motion [8], multi-agent robotics [9], and electricity markets [10]. Inverse problems of this type have been widely studied in the single-agent setting under inverse optimal control (IOC) and inverse reinforcement learning (IRL); see [11]‚Äď [13]. Extending these frameworks to multi-agent settings leads to inverse dynamic games (IDGs), where identification may be model-based, assuming known dynamics, or model-free, estimating both dynamics and objectives. Molloy et al. [14], [15] propose identification schemes for finite-horizon IDGs by reformulating optimality conditions derived from the Pontrya-gin maximum principle. For infinite-horizon linear‚Äďquadratic differential games (LQDGs) under a feedback information structure, Rothfu√ü et al. [16] propose IOC-based methods. For this class of games, Inga et al. [17] characterize the solution sets by reformulating the coupled algebraic Riccati equations, while a frequency-domain approach is proposed in [18]. Martirosyan et al. [19], [20] develop model-based

IRL and model-free identification methods for infinite-horizon LQDGs under a feedback information structure. Most existing IDG formulations assume that agents‚Äô inter-actions are governed by differential or difference equations. However, in many of the application domains mentioned above, multi-agent systems are subject to instantaneous al-gebraic constraints arising from physical interconnections or conservation laws, as in power networks and constrained robotic systems. Since these constraints may couple state and control variables, such systems are more naturally modeled by differential-algebraic equations (DAEs). This distinction is particularly important in the inverse setting, where inferred objectives are sensitive to model misspecification. If interac-tions on a constraint manifold are modeled by unconstrained dynamics, behavior enforced by feasibility constraints may be incorrectly attributed to strategic preferences, leading to cost reconstructions that are difficult to interpret. A DAE-based inverse game framework therefore makes it possible to sep-arate behavior dictated by constraints from behavior induced by strategic preferences, enabling recovery of objectives con-sistent with the true interaction structure. Although descriptor systems have been studied in forward linear-quadratic Nash games [21]‚Äď[23], inverse formulations for such systems have not been developed, to the best of our knowledge. The main contribution of this letter is the formulation and solution of a model-based inverse dynamic game in which the agents‚Äô interaction environment is described by a DAE. We consider infinite-horizon linear-quadratic descriptor differential games (LQDDGs) under a feedback information pattern. Our objective is to identify all cost parameters that rationalize an observed feedback strategy profile of the players as a feedback Nash equilibrium (FBNE). To this end, we build on the necessary and sufficient conditions for FBNE existence in LQDDGs [21], [22], which reduce the game to a standard LQDG on a low-dimensional constraint manifold and require the solution of coupled algebraic Riccati equations. By reformulating these conditions, we characterize the inverse solution set and show that it is rectangular and convex, analogous to the inverse LQDG result in [17]. We also provide a convex optimization approach to compute an element of the solution set whenever it is nonempty. This letter is organized as follows. In Section II, we provide preliminaries and inverse problem formulation. In Section III, we characterize the solution set associated with the inverse problem and analyze its structure. In Section IV, we illustrate our approach for identification with numerical simulations, and finally Section V concludes.

V. C ONCLUSIONS 

In this paper, we formulated and solved an inverse prob-lem for infinite-horizon linear‚Äďquadratic descriptor differential games. We characterized the set of all cost functions that rationalize an observed feedback strategy profile as a feedback Nash equilibrium. We established key structural properties of this set and proposed an algorithm for computing a feasible solution. A natural direction for future work is to extend the proposed framework to a model-free setting.
