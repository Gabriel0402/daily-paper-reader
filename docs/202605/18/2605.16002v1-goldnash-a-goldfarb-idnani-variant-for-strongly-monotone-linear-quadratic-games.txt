# GoldNash: A Goldfarb-Idnani Variant for Strongly Monotone Linear-Quadratic Games 

Alberto Bemporad *

May 18, 2026 

Abstract 

We present a simple variant of the Goldfarb-Idnani (GI) dual active-set method for computing variational generalized Nash equilibria of strongly monotone N -player games with convex quadratic costs and shared affine in-equality and equality constraints. We prove that the original properties of the algorithm are maintained in spite of having a possibly non-symmetric pseudogradient matrix in the joint KKT system of the game. Our numerical results show that the method, which we call GoldNash, most often outper-forms alternative state-of-the-art algorithms and enables very efficient com-putations of game-theoretic linear model predictive control laws. A Python implementation of the algorithm is available in the open-source NashOpt 

library. 

# 1 Introduction 

Linear-quadratic generalized Nash equilibrium problems (LQ-GNEPs) arise when multiple decision makers optimize convex quadratic objectives under local and shared affine constraints. This structure is central in game-theoretic model pre-dictive control (GT-MPC) of linear systems, where finite-horizon dynamic games must be solved repeatedly and reliably online in fast-sampling applications, such as unmanned aerial vehicles [7], autonomous driving [1], and demand-side energy management [12]. In the presence of shared constraints, a common approach is to seek a varia-tional generalized Nash equilibrium (v-GNE), in which all players assign the same multiplier to the common constraints [21]. Indeed, most of the existing algorithms for solving GNEPs are designed to compute v-GNEs, as conditions of existence and uniqueness of solutions can be well characterized [10].

Several numerical methods exist for solving LQ-GNEPs. Since, under inequal-ity constraints, their variational equilibria can be characterized as solutions of an affine variational inequality (AVI), or equivalently as a linear complementarity problem (LCP) with possibly non-symmetric LCP matrix, pivotal methods have been proposed for AVI/LCP reformulations [5, 15, 22]. Interior-point methods are another important class of algorithms for solving classes of GNEPs [8], and LQ-GNEPs in particular [18]. Splitting-based operator frameworks were also proposed to solve v-GNEs [2,4, 25], see also the recent package [19] collecting several of them; such methods typ-ically have inexpensive iterations and favorable communication structure, but their convergence is asymptotic, which can be restrictive for embedded GT-MPC where highly accurate solutions are usually required at each sampling instant within short time intervals. The hybrid active-set method DR-DAQP was recently proposed in [1] to effectively combine the robustness of Douglas-Rachford iterations with the finite termination properties of active-set methods. Augmented Lagrangian formulations were proposed in [14, 16] and distributed algorithms in [2,23–25]. A learning-based approach relying on best-response mea-surements was proposed in [9]. For parametric (possibly non-variational) LQ-GNEPs of moderate size, multiparametric programming was proposed recently in [13] to explicitly characterize the (possibly multiple) solutions as a function of the parameters. LQ-GNEPs can also be solved by reformulating the KKT conditions as a mixed-integer linear or quadratic program (MILP/MIQP) [3], which, although often nu-merically inefficient, enables finding non-variational GNEs and extracting multiple equilibria. General-purpose numerical approaches that minimize the residuals of the KKT conditions [3, 8] can also be used, in principle, to seek (variational) equi-libria of LQ-GNEPs, although they are not guaranteed in general to converge to a GNE and are rather inefficient due to the use of (nonconvex) nonlinear program-ming. 

1.1 Contribution 

Motivated by real-time GT-MPC applications in which repeated equilibrium com-putations must be both fast and accurate, in this paper we adapt the Goldfarb-Idnani (GI) dual active-set algorithm [11], designed for symmetric positive-definite quadratic programming (QP) problems, to the non-symmetric, strongly monotone affine pseudogradient appearing in the VI/KKT characterization of a v-GNE. The resulting method, which we call GoldNash, enjoys the finite-termination, high-accuracy, and low-cost working-set update properties of GI. A Python implemen-tation of the method is available in the NashOpt library [3]. The rest of the paper is organized as follows. In Section 2 we formulate the problem of computing v-GNEs of LQ-GNEPs and in Section 3 we analyze relevant properties of the associated pseudogradient matrix. In Section 4 we present the GoldNash algorithm and prove its correctness. In Section 5 we present numerical 2results comparing GoldNash with alternative state-of-the-art algorithms for solving LQ-GNEPs, including those arising in GT-MPC problems. Finally, we draw some conclusions in Section 6.

# 5 Numerical Results 

We compare the performance of Algorithm 1 (labeled as g*nash ) against alterna-tive methods for computing v-GNEs of strongly monotone LQ games: the proximal ADMM method of [4] ( admm ) extended to handle inequalities as described in [3, Section 4.2.1]; the log-barrier interior-point method of [18] ( ipm ); the DR-DAQP method of [1] ( daqp ); and Lemke’s algorithm applied to the primal ( lemke ) and dual ( lemke D) LCP reformulations described in the following Section 5.1. All these methods, including a Python implementation of GoldNash, are implemented in or interfaced with the NashOpt library and called with default options. We do not include MILP-based methods to solve the KKT system (3) as described in [3], as they are mainly conceived to solve non-variational and possibly non-monotone LQ-GNEPs, nor KKT-residual methods, as they would be inefficient for the con-sidered class of GNEPs.

# 6 Conclusions 

We presented GoldNash, an efficient active-set method for computing variational generalized Nash equilibria of strongly monotone N -player linear-quadratic games with shared affine constraints. We verified that the key algebraic ingredients of the Goldfarb-Idnani algorithm, of which GoldNash is just a variant, carry over from the symmetric QP case to the variational LQ-GNE setting without modification to the constraint add/drop logic. Numerical experiments on random LQ games and a GT-MPC benchmark have shown that GoldNash often outperforms Lemke, ADMM, interior-point, and DR-DAQP solvers for moderate-to-large problem sizes, with a clear scaling advantage as the number of agents and constraints grows.
