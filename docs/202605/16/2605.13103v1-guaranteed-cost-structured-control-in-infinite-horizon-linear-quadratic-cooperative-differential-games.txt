# Guaranteed cost structured control in infinite-horizon linear-quadratic cooperative differential games 

Aniruddha Roy and Pavankumar Tallapragada, Member, IEEE 

Abstract — In this letter, we consider infinite-horizon linear-quadratic cooperative differential games with output feedback information structure. We first demonstrate that, under output feedback information structure, computing Pareto optimal controls can be difficult even for simple low-dimensional differential games. To address this issue, this letter introduces the concept of feedback guaranteed cost structured control (GCSC). The feedback GCSC concept is inspired from suboptimal control. At a feedback GCSC, the total weighted team cost remains below a prescribed threshold while satisfying the structural constraints. We derive fundamental properties of the feedback GCSC and the admissible weight set, including their monotonicity properties. In particular, we show that if Pareto optimal controls exist, they belong to the class of feedback GCSCs. We also quantify the suboptimalty of Pareto optimal con-trols (if they exist) and the proposed GCSC with respect to output feedback optimal control. Furthermore, we provide the conditions for verification and the synthesis of a feed-back GCSC. Finally, we illustrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach through numerical examples, including a case study on tracking synchronization in a microgrid. 

Index Terms — Game theory, Cooperative control, Differ-ential games, Linear-matrix inequalities (LMIs) 

I. I NTRODUCTION 

Differential game theory provides a mathematical frame-work for analyzing strategic interactions among multiple play-ers [1]. A cooperative differential game (CDG) arises when players coordinate their control actions to optimize a collective objective [2], [3]. Pareto optimality has been widely studied in CDGs, with applications in areas such as power systems [4], [5], human-robot coordination [6], and consensus problems [7]. Pareto optimality under an open-loop information struc-ture in a finite-horizon setting was studied in [8] and later extended to the infinite-horizon case in [9], [10]. In particular, [9] established necessary and sufficient conditions for Pareto optimality in infinite-horizon linear-quadratic cooperative dif-ferential games (LQ-CDGs) and showed its connection to a weighted-sum optimal control problem. Despite these advances, many real-world systems consist of multiple interacting agents, commonly referred to as multi-agent systems (MAS). In such systems, agents are often

heterogeneous and communicate over a directed network, where each agent typically has access only to local (or output) information. Work in [7] primarily addressed consensus in homogeneous MAS with decoupled dynamics under an undi-rected graph. Moreover, [9], [10] studied the Pareto optimality problem under an open-loop information structure. However, the problem of obtaining Pareto optimal solutions in a CDG setting under output feedback information remains largely unexplored. We note that the control of heterogeneous MAS using the guaranteed cost equilibrium concept was studied for networked differential games in a non-cooperative setting in [11], and later extended to a general class of linear-quadratic non-cooperative differential games (LQ-NCDGs) with output feedback information structure in [12]. The focus of the present work is fundamentally different from [12]. Specifically, [12] studies an equilibrium concept in an LQ-NCDG setting, whereas the current work addresses a cooperative control problem in an LQ-CDG framework under an output-feedback information structure. To address the above-mentioned gaps in the existing lit-erature, we propose a feedback guaranteed cost structured control (GCSC) concept for infinite-horizon LQ-CDGs with output feedback information structure. Note that, we consider a general class of LQ-CDGs with non-transferable utility (NTU), where all players form the grand coalition. The formation of sub-coalitions and utility transfers (side payments) dur-ing the game are not considered; see [13], [14] for related discussions. Inspired from suboptimal control [15]–[17], the objective is to design a structured cooperative controller that ensures the weighted team cost remains below a prescribed bound while satisfying the structural constraint. To the best of our knowledge, this problem has not been studied in the existing literature. The main contributions of this letter are: (i) we introduce the notion of feedback GCSC for a general class of LQ-CDG (ii) we derive fundamental properties of the feedback GCSC and the admissible weight set, including monotonicity (Lemma 2) and the inclusion of Pareto optimal controls within the GCSC class (Theorem 1), (iii) we quantify the associated performance measures (Lemma 3), and (iv) we provide verification (Theorem 2) and synthesis (Theorem 3) conditions for a feedback GCSC. Remark 7. While Theorem 3 and [12, Theorem 5.7] are both synthesis results derived using fundamental results from the LMI literature, namely the projection lemma and the Schur complement lemma [22], they differ fundamentally in scope and computational complexity. Specifically, Theorem 3 addresses the synthesis problem in an LQ-CDG framework, whereas [12, Theorem 5.7] considers an LQ-NCDG setting. We note that the feasibility conditions in [12, Theorem 5.7, Eq. (27e)] must be verified separately for each of the N

players. Furthermore, since the resulting feasibility set in [12, Theorem 5.7, Eq. (27e)] is non-convex, a semi-definite programming (SDP) relaxation is required. Consequently, the solution procedure depends on the sequential linear program-ming matrix method (SLPMM); see [12, Section 6.2] for details. In contrast, the set (16) associated with Theorem 3 is convex. As a result, neither SDP relaxation nor SLPMM is required, and the feasibility condition needs to be verified only once. This clearly demonstrates the significant computa-tional advantage of Theorem 3 within the cooperative game framework.

VI. C ONCLUSION 

In this letter, we developed the notion of feedback GCSC for infinite-horizon LQ-CDGs. At a feedback GCSC, the weighted team cost is upper bounded by a prescribed threshold while satisfying the structural constraints. We established several fundamental properties of the feedback GCSC and the admissible weight set. In particular, we showed that if Pareto optimal controls exist, then they belong to the class of feedback GCSCs. In addition, we provided performance measures for the Pareto optimal controls (whenever they exist) and the proposed GCSC relative to the output feedback optimal control. Furthermore, verification and synthesis results were derived using LMIs, where the synthesis formulation is convex and requires no SDP relaxation. For future work, it would be interesting to study the proposed GCSC concept for scalar CDGs and stochastic CDGs.
