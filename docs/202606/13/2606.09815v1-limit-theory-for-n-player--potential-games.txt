# Limit Theory for N -Player α-Potential Games 

Xin Guo ∗ Meng Wang † Yufei Zhang †

Abstract 

The framework of α-potential games has recently been introduced as a tool to analyze finite-player dynamic games, reducing the challenging task of finding approximate Nash equi-libria to a control problem of minimizing a single function called α-potential function. In this work, we investigate the limiting behavior of α-potential games as the number of players N

tends to infinity. We show that potential mean field games (MFGs) arise naturally as this limit. Specifically, both the optimal values and the minimizers of normalized N -player αN -potential functions converge to those of a mean field control (MFC) problem with measure-valued con-trols. We establish the equivalence of lim N →∞ αN = 0 with the existing conditions for potential MFGs, and provide an unified approach to construct the potential function for MFGs using the techniques from differential geometry in Wasserstein space. We further demonstrate that the objective of the limiting MFC problem serves as a potential function for the correspond-ing MFGs, an extension of the analogous finite-player setting. This connection yields new constructions of potential MFGs from a finite-player game, through the asymptotic condition lim N →∞ αN = 0. As a by-product, we establish propagation of chaos for N -player games converging to MFGs for general controlled diffusions with common noise and non-separable control interactions. 

Key words. α-potential game, potential mean field game of control, mean field control, measure-valued control, Poincar´ e lemma on Wasserstein space, propagation of chaos

# 1 Introduction 

Potential games and α-potential games. Potential games provide a powerful framework for analyzing strategic interactions among multiple agents by reducing equilibrium computation to the optimization of a single global objective, known as a potential function. Since the seminal work of Monderer and Shapley [1], potential structures have played a central role in game theory, optimization, and learning in multi-agent systems. In stochastic differential games, however, identifying exact potential structures remains challenging due to the coupling between players’ controls and state dynamics [2]. Recent work by [3] introduces the framework of α-potential game, which relaxes the exact potential condition by allowing a controlled approximation error α. This framework has proven effective for studying approximate Nash equilibria (NEs) in finite-player dynamic games, transforming the equilibrium problem into the minimization of an α-potential function [4, 5, 6, 7, 8].

MFGs and potential MFGs. At the same time, mean field game (MFG) theory provides a tractable asymptotic framework for studying strategic interactions among a large number of weakly 

interacting homogeneous agents. By passing to the limit as the number of players tends to infin-ity, MFGs replace finite-player interactions with interactions through the population distribution, leading to equilibrium problems on the space of probability measures. A particularly relevant sub-class is that of potential MFGs, where the equilibrium can be characterized through a mean field control (MFC) problem. In this framework of potential MFGs [9], potential functions are defined as antiderivatives of the running and terminal cost functions. (see e.g., [10, 9, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15]). This appears conceptually different from the definition in N -player potential games [1, 2]. Re-cently, by analyzing potential MFGs through the MFC framework, [15] shows that the perturba-tion of the representative player’s objective in the mean-field setting equals the derivative of the mean-field potential function along the interpolation path, moving a step forward in narrowing the conceptual gap between the two potential-game frameworks. The natural next step is to understand the precise relationship between finite-player α-potential games and potential MFGs. This is the goal of our paper. 

Our work. This paper establishes a rigorous connection between these two frameworks, by studying the limiting behavior of N -player αN -potential games as N → ∞ and show that potential MFGs arise naturally as the limit when lim N →∞ αN = 0. This connection yields new constructions of potential MFGs from a finite-player game, through the asymptotic condition lim N →∞ αN = 0. It provides a new route to propagation of chaos for N -player games: the limit of αN -NE as the minimizer of some associated αN -potential function is an NE of the limiting MFG. This result applies to general controlled diffusions with common noise and non-separable nonlinear state–control interactions. Specifically, 

• We present a new construction of αN -potential functions for a class of N -player stochastic differential games in which each player’s state follows a controlled diffusion driven by idiosyncratic and common noise. The drift and diffusion coefficients depend nonlinearly on the player’s own state and control, while the cost depends on all players’ states and controls. The resulting αN -potential function is formulated as a finite-dimensional control problem, and the corresponding approximation error αN is characterized analytically in Theorem 2.1. Unlike existing constructions [3, 5, 16] based on derivarive of the controlled state processes with respect to the control, this new construction exploits the decoupled structure of the players’ state dynamics and constructs the potential through path integrals in an enlarged state-control space. It avoids the previous infinite-dimensional control formulation of α-potential functions, accommodates Lipschitz continuous state coefficients, and facilitates a more tractable analysis of the corresponding αN -NEs and their asymptotic behavior as the number of players grows. 

• We analyze the large-population limit of the N -player αN -potential functions, by adopt-ing the propagation-of-chaos framework [17, 18]. We show that, after normalizing the N -player 

αN -potential function by N , both the optimal values and the approximate minimizers of the normalized αN -potential functions converge to those of an MFC problem on a lifted space of measure-valued controls (Theorem 3.1). The lifted control space involves a hierarchy of probabil-ity measures, capturing three effects separately: idiosyncratic randomness, temporal oscillations of control processes, and common noise or additional randomness appearing in the limit. This lifting of the control space for the limiting MFC problem follows closely the work of [17], except that the N -player αN -potential functions involve cost functions that depend explicitly on the number of players N and do not coincide with the cost functions in the limiting MFC problem. 2Consistent with [17], this lifting is essential due to the nonlinear dependence of the running cost on the control distribution. Different from [17], we show that such a lifting is necessary even when the state dynamics are independent of the population distribution, in order to capture all limits of approximate minimizers of N -player αN -potential functions. This point is demonstrated through several examples in Section 3.4, both with and without common noise. 

• We establish equivalent characterizations of lim N →∞ αN = 0 (Theorems 4.1 and Theorem 4.2). In particular, we show its equivalence to the standard conditions imposed for potential MFGs, thereby demonstrating that these conditions arise naturally as limits of N -player αN -potential games with vanishing αN . These characterizations also extend previous results on po-tential functions for N -player games [1, 2] for cost functions with symmetric Hessians. Technically, these results are Poincar´ e lemma on the Wasserstein space, stating that suitable closed differential forms on this space are exact, i.e., they coincide with the differential of a function on the Wasserstein space, which in turn is given by path integrals of the differential form. The proof relies on extending Green’s theorem in [19] to less regular differential forms on the Wasserstein space. (See Proposition 5.1 and Remark 5.1). 

• Under the condition lim N →∞ αN = 0, we prove that the objective functional of the MFC problem, namely the limit of the normalized N -player αN -potential functions, serves as a potential function for an associated MFG with measure-valued controls (Theorem 4.3). We further prove that the αN -NEs of N -player games that minimize the αN -potential functions converge to a mean field equilibrium of the MFG. This result holds for general controlled diffusions with common noise and non-separable state–control interactions in the cost functions (Corollary 4.1). Our work differs from the earlier work on potential MFGs (see e.g., [10, 9, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15]) in two main respects: First, existing works focus on MFGs with infinitely many players, and impose conditions directly on the cost functions to connect them with an MFC problem. In contrast, we show that potential MFGs arise naturally as the limits of N -player αN -potential games (Remark 4.4). Specifically, we start from an N -player game, identify the limit of the αN -potential functions as the objective of an MFC problem, and further show that this objective is a potential function for an MFG. Our work presents an explicit way to construct a potential MFG from a finite-player game, through the asymptotic condition lim N →∞ αN = 0. Second, existing works focus on potential MFGs with strict controls; we instead develop the theory for potential MFGs with measure-valued controls. As discussed earlier, this extension is essential for ensuring that limits of approximate NEs of the N -player games correspond to equilibria of the MFG. 

Organization of the paper. Section 2 introduces the finite-player stochastic differential game and constructs the associated αN -potential function. Section 3 studies the large-population limit and establishes the convergence to an MFC problem with measure-valued controls. Section 4 proves the equivalence between lim N →∞ αN = 0 and classical potential MFG conditions, provides a unified construction of mean field potential functions, and shows the consistency between the limiting MFC problem and potential MFGs. Proofs of main results are in Section 5.

3.4 Necessity of lifted control spaces in the limit

Theorem 4.2 extends the char-acterization of potential functions for N -player static games in [1, Theorem 4.5] and N -player dynamic games in [2, Theorem 3.2].
