Abstract 

Open-world interpretation aims to accurately localize and recognize all objects within images by vision-language models (VLMs). While substantial progress has been made in this task for natural images, the advancements for re-mote sensing (RS) images still remain limited, primarily due to these two challenges. 1) Existing RS semantic cat-egories are limited, particularly for pixel-level interpreta-tion datasets. 2) Distinguishing among diverse RS spa-tial regions solely by language space is challenging due to the dense and intricate spatial distribution in open-world RS imagery. To address the first issue, we develop a fine-grained RS interpretation dataset, Sky-SA, which contains 183,375 high-quality local image-text pairs with full-pixel manual annotations, covering 1,763 category labels, ex-hibiting richer semantics and higher density than previ-ous datasets. Afterwards, to solve the second issue, we introduce the vision-centric principle for vision-language modeling. Specifically, in the pre-training stage, the vi-sual self-supervised paradigm is incorporated into image-

Then, we construct a visual-relevance knowledge graph across open-category texts and further develop a novel vision-centric image-text contrastive loss for fine-tuning with text prompts. This new model, denoted as SkySense-O, demonstrates im-pressive zero-shot capabilities on a thorough evaluation encompassing 14 datasets over 4 tasks, from recognizing to reasoning and classification to localization. Specifi-cally, it outperforms the latest models such as SegEarth-OV, GeoRSCLIP, and VHM by a large margin, i.e., 11.95%, 8.04% and 3.55% on average respectively. The code is publicly available to facilitate further research at https: //github.com/zqcrafts/SkySense-O. 

1. Introduction 

Remote sensing interpretation (RSI) is a critical and prac-tical task in Earth observation, aiming to identify the se-mantic types of specific ground objects in remote sensing (RS) images (such as segmentation [1–4], detection [5–10], change detection [11, 12], and visual question answering This CVPR paper is the Open Access version, provided by the Computer Vision Foundation. Except for this watermark, it is identical to the accepted version; the final published version of the proceedings is available on IEEE Xplore. 14733 (VQA) [13–15]). Early works typically involved annotating RS images with fixed categories and exploring RSI through supervised learning methods. These approaches do not gen-eralize well to unseen new categories in open-world sce-narios, thus limiting their practical applicability. Moreover, as RS images becoming more accessible than ever before, there is an increasing demand for open-world interpreta-tions with a broad category scope. Hence, there is a soar-ing interest in exploring a comprehensive open-world re-mote sensing interpretation model for many scenarios and tasks [16, 17]. 

Challenges. In the domain of natural image scenes, signif-icant progress has been achieved in open-world interpreta-tion tasks through large-scale localization and recognition datasets like AS-1B [18] and COCO-Stuff [19], combined with contrastive language-image pretrained model (CLIP) shown in Fig. 1a. However, the open-world RS interpreta-tion model is still out of reach due to following two reasons: 

1) From the dataset perspective, the existing annotations ei-ther exhibit sparse mask labels or lack diversity in category descriptions (see Fig.1b). 2) From the modeling perspec-tive, the language model inherently encompasses strong contextual dependence [20], making it challenging to di-rectly use as prompts for decoupling common RS objects and intertwined land-cover categories in open-world. 

Sky-SA Dataset. For Struggle (1) , inspired by SAM [21] and ASM [18] in the natural image domain, we propose the Sky-SA dataset with the largest number of category la-bels for open-world RS interpretation, including 1,763 cat-egory texts from 183,375 mask-text pairs. The Sky-SA is mainly characterized by its text openness and fine granu-larity (Fig. 1b). Specifically, different from general seman-tic segmentation datasets, the categories of Sky-SA are an-notated by short semantic tags in a completely open man-ner. In other words, the annotators are doing fill-in-the-blank rather than multiple-choice. This enables the model to learn pixel-level open-world interpretation akin to human cognition [18] (Fig. 1c). Furthermore, similar to COCO-Stuff [19], all recognizable pixels of Sky-SA are densely annotated, which further enhances the semantic openness and the contextual relationships from wide semantic cate-gories. 

Vision-Centric Modeling. For Struggle (2) , motivated by [22], we introduce the vision-centric principle to correct the potential alignment ambiguity arising from language guidance. Firstly, we initialize the visual encoder from Sky-Sense [23], an advanced RS visual foundation model, and then collect large-scale RS image-caption datasets for pre-training, which mainly include SkyScript [23], RS5M [24] and OSM [25]. Motivated by DetailCLIP [26], to miti-gate the degradation of general visual representation ca-pabilities originating from SkySense, we maintain the vi-sual contrastive loss and masked modeling loss during OpenStreetMap

3. Sky-SA Dataset 

3.1. Motivation 

Recently, image-text alignment models based on natural images, such as the open-vocabulary methods [75] and MLLMs like LLaVA [60], have achieved remarkable suc-cess. This is primarily due to well-constructed datasets in the natural image domain compared to the RS image domain. There have been numerous high-quality datasets with rich categories in the natural image community, such as COCO-Stuff [19] and ADE [76]. However, as shown in Tab.1 and Fig.4, such resources remain scarce in the RS community, which significantly limits the development of open-world RS interpretation tasks. Moreover, existing open-world models, such as SAM [21], Florence-2 [77], and Grounding-DINO [78], are also unable to achieve dense and fine-grained interpretation on remote sensing images as shown in Fig. 3. To relieve this issue, we design a data en-gine to help annotators more efficiently and accurately per-form full-pixel annotation on unlabeled RS images, leading to the creation of the Sky-SA dataset as following section. 

3.2. Data Engine 

Open-Images Collection. In the standard collection pro-cess of a closed-set dataset, a category list is predefined, and images that do not contain any items from this list will be filtered out. This manner might lead to dataset bias [79] in remote sensing images. Unlike the close-set methods, we compile a comprehensive collection of images from various available RS datasets. To minimize redundancy in manual annotation, we leverage the SkySense model [23], which possesses image-level visual discrimination capabilities for RS images, to filter visually redundant images. 

Open-Text Fine-Grained Annotation. In the annota-tion process, drawing inspiration from the popular COCO-Stuff [19] dataset, we prioritize fine-grained semantics and 14735 Text

Dataset Statistics. Finally, we develop a high-quality pixel-level remote sensing interpretation dataset, named Sky-SA. As shown in Fig. 4, the Sky-SA dataset consists of 35,000 RS images, mainly including 1,763 category texts from 183,375 segment-text pairs (encompassing approximately 500 distinct categories of land-covering and RS objects af-ter synonym filtering), which far surpasses existing remote sensing segmentation datasets. Moreover, Sky-SA is also the first pixel-level open-text annotated dataset in the field of remote sensing (see Fig. 1), laying a solid data founda-tion for the development of open-world RS interpretation.

4.3. Vision-Centric Modeling 

Challenge. Different from the previous close-set seman-tic segmentation dataset, the Sky-SA dataset is annotated in open-world texts. Although this manner provides more open vocabulary support, the ambiguous sampling chal-lenge often leads to training collapse in discriminative mod-els. As illustrated in Fig. 6, “car” is mistakenly regarded as the positive sample of “road” by computing the similarity between their text-embeddings due to their strong text-level or image-level contextual relationships. 

Visual-Centric Knowledge Graph. To overcome the chal-lenge, inspired by the in-depth exploration of visual repre-sentation by Xie et al. [22] and GraphRAG [81], we build a visual-centric agent using GPT-4 to obtain the visual rel-evance score for each text pair of the Sky-SA dataset. As shown in Fig. 7, the score is measured by the meticulously chain of thought about visual attributes, including appear-ance, color, and shape etc. . When all text pairs in Sky-SA are fully traversed, we can construct an offline vision-centric knowledge graph, which mainly consists of three el-ements: source node, target node, and edge. The edge in-dicates the visual-relevance score and reasons from source node to target node. Vision-Centric Regularization Loss. In RS imagery, the objects and land-cover are often densely packed and in-tricately intertwined, posing challenges for fine-grained visual-language modeling. To relieve this issue, we further exploit the potential of the vision-centric graph and design a vision-centric regularization loss, Lvc . 6. Conclusion 

In this paper, we present Skysense-O, a large-scale and general remote sensing interpretation model for open re-mote sensing images. Skysense-O is mainly driven by the proposed semantically-rich and fine-grained dataset Sky-SA and the vision-centric visual-language modeling, con-sistently outperforming other models on a variety of tasks, showcasing its exceptional generalization ability and strong performance. We hope this work will constructively push the RS open-world interpretation on constructing large-scale vision-language datasets and models for further re-searches. Our future work aims to explore fine-grained re-mote sensing interpretation, such as analyzing object states and relationships, further enhancing the flexibility of open-world applications. 14740 Acknowledgments
